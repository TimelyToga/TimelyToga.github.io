## Ranked Favorites (Top 5)

<div class="book-entry">
  <img src="https://covers.openlibrary.org/b/isbn/9780961392147-M.jpg" alt="The Visual Display of Quantitative Information" class="book-cover-small">
  <div class="book-info">
    <h3>1. The Visual Display of Quantitative Information</h3>
    <div class="book-author">Edward Tufte</div>
    <p class="book-notes">Probably the best book I read all year. Was really surprised by how much I enjoyed it. But I see Throne as a UI company for a proprietary dataset, so communicating information to our customers is one of the most important jobs we have.</p>
  </div>
</div>

<div class="book-entry">
  <img src="https://covers.openlibrary.org/b/isbn/9781982181284-M.jpg" alt="Elon Musk" class="book-cover-small">
  <div class="book-info">
    <h3>2. Elon Musk</h3>
    <div class="book-author">Walter Isaacson</div>
    <p class="book-notes">Stressful look at the life of one of the most stressed people. Isaacson does a great job of bringing you into Musk's world and does his best to draw a link between the events and situations that shaped him and his evolving approaches to problems over time.</p>
  </div>
</div>

<div class="book-entry">
  <img src="https://covers.openlibrary.org/b/isbn/9780063046061-M.jpg" alt="Build" class="book-cover-small">
  <div class="book-info">
    <h3>3. Build</h3>
    <div class="book-author">Tony Fadell</div>
    <p class="book-notes">One of the best books about building hardware companies I have ever read. Really enjoyed this. Would recommend to anyone building a hardware business.</p>
  </div>
</div>

<div class="book-entry">
  <img src="https://covers.openlibrary.org/b/isbn/9780465026562-M.jpg" alt="Gödel, Escher, Bach" class="book-cover-small">
  <div class="book-info">
    <h3>4. Gödel, Escher, Bach</h3>
    <div class="book-author">Douglas Hofstadter</div>
    <p class="book-notes">Tough to start, hard to put down. I lamented that this author refused to sell a digital version of their book, but this is less of a novel and more of an enjoyable textbook. Lots of fun math ideas explained gently. "Strange loops" introduce a lot of interesting problems for formal systems! When reading this book, I would ruminate on 2-3 page sections for a long while to make sure I could play the ideas out. Really fun read.</p>
  </div>
</div>

<div class="book-entry">
  <img src="https://covers.openlibrary.org/b/isbn/9781476766683-M.jpg" alt="A Mind at Play" class="book-cover-small">
  <div class="book-info">
    <h3>5. A Mind at Play</h3>
    <div class="book-author">Rob Goodman</div>
    <p class="book-notes">Wonderful biography of Claude Shannon, the "Father of the Information Age". Wanders in the middle a bit, but does a great job pulling the threads together around why Information Theory is non-obvious and an extremely useful underpinning of computer science.</p>
  </div>
</div>
