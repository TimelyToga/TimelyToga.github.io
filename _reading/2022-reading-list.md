## Ranked Favorites (Top 15)

<div class="book-entry">
  <img src="https://covers.openlibrary.org/b/isbn/9781591846604-M.jpg" alt="The Smartest Guys in the Room" class="book-cover-small">
  <div class="book-info">
    <h3>1. The Smartest Guys in the Room</h3>
    <div class="book-author">Bethany McLean</div>
    <p class="book-notes">Was halfway through when the bottom fell out of FTX. McLean gives you the information to identify the grift, but fails to actually point the finger at Skilling and Lay and call a spade a grifting fraudster.</p>
  </div>
</div>

<div class="book-entry">
  <img src="https://covers.openlibrary.org/b/isbn/9781591391821-M.jpg" alt="The First 90 Days" class="book-cover-small">
  <div class="book-info">
    <h3>2. The First 90 Days</h3>
    <div class="book-author">Michael Watkins</div>
    <p class="book-notes">A playbook for effective leadership. Identifies the common failure modes of new executives. Biggest takeaway: what made you successful in your last job will definitely not make you successful in your next one.</p>
  </div>
</div>

<div class="book-entry">
  <img src="https://covers.openlibrary.org/b/isbn/9780393338690-M.jpg" alt="Liar's Poker" class="book-cover-small">
  <div class="book-info">
    <h3>3. Liar's Poker</h3>
    <div class="book-author">Michael Lewis</div>
    <p class="book-notes">Lewis claims he wasn't trying to get college kids excited to go into finance but that's clearly bullshit. High-flying tales of chaotic bond trading in the 1980s.</p>
  </div>
</div>

<div class="book-entry">
  <img src="https://covers.openlibrary.org/b/isbn/9780393338829-M.jpg" alt="The Big Short" class="book-cover-small">
  <div class="book-info">
    <h3>4. The Big Short</h3>
    <div class="book-author">Michael Lewis</div>
    <p class="book-notes">Unbelievable that I didn't realize ML's genius until 2022. A timeless masterpiece. Also made me realize how unhinged Michael Burry is. I would have lost my mind if Scion Capital had my money.</p>
  </div>
</div>

<div class="book-entry">
  <img src="https://covers.openlibrary.org/b/isbn/9780375758256-M.jpg" alt="When Genius Failed" class="book-cover-small">
  <div class="book-info">
    <h3>5. When Genius Failed</h3>
    <div class="book-author">Roger Lowenstein</div>
    <p class="book-notes">Great follow-up to Liar's Poker because John Meriwether was first introduced there. A perfect model performing well and bad model performing well are indistinguishable until your firm is entirely underwater.</p>
  </div>
</div>

<div class="book-entry">
  <img src="https://covers.openlibrary.org/b/isbn/9781616202811-M.jpg" alt="The Shift" class="book-cover-small">
  <div class="book-info">
    <h3>6. The Shift</h3>
    <div class="book-author">Theresa Brown</div>
    <p class="book-notes">Wasn't expecting to find a book about a single nurse's shift at a hospital to be so touching and beautiful, but I ripped through this book in only a few days. Nurses are heroes.</p>
  </div>
</div>

<div class="book-entry">
  <img src="https://covers.openlibrary.org/b/isbn/9781591845638-M.jpg" alt="The Outsiders" class="book-cover-small">
  <div class="book-info">
    <h3>7. The Outsiders</h3>
    <div class="book-author">William N. Thorndike</div>
    <p class="book-notes">Portraits of iconoclasts innovating business. Great long-term looks at excellent businessmen executing at incredibly high levels (without the support of their contemporaries).</p>
  </div>
</div>

<div class="book-entry">
  <img src="https://covers.openlibrary.org/b/isbn/9780998116310-M.jpg" alt="7 Powers" class="book-cover-small">
  <div class="book-info">
    <h3>8. 7 Powers</h3>
    <div class="book-author">Hamilton Helmer</div>
    <p class="book-notes">My second attempt at this book, but I was mired in enough business books this year that my brain could understand the content of the book.</p>
  </div>
</div>

<div class="book-entry">
  <img src="https://covers.openlibrary.org/b/isbn/9780684845593-M.jpg" alt="Hardball" class="book-cover-small">
  <div class="book-info">
    <h3>9. Hardball</h3>
    <div class="book-author">Chris Matthews</div>
    <p class="book-notes">A series of discontinuous stories that illustrate how political power is fought for, won, and leveraged in the United States.</p>
  </div>
</div>

<div class="book-entry">
  <img src="https://covers.openlibrary.org/b/isbn/9780393351590-M.jpg" alt="Flash Boys" class="book-cover-small">
  <div class="book-info">
    <h3>10. Flash Boys</h3>
    <div class="book-author">Michael Lewis</div>
    <p class="book-notes">Discover HFT with a kindly Canadian protagonist from RBC that just wants the financial markets to be fair (he's super shocked they aren't).</p>
  </div>
</div>

<div class="book-entry">
  <img src="https://covers.openlibrary.org/b/isbn/9781324092032-M.jpg" alt="The Metaverse" class="book-cover-small">
  <div class="book-info">
    <h3>11. The Metaverse</h3>
    <div class="book-author">Matthew Ball</div>
    <p class="book-notes">Made me feel that vibrant, boyish enthusiasm for technology that I first felt buzzing around SF as a 19 year old. Exploration of what a metaverse could be and the analysis of the people working on different approaches.</p>
  </div>
</div>

<div class="book-entry">
  <img src="https://covers.openlibrary.org/b/isbn/9780061655555-M.jpg" alt="Barbarians at the Gate" class="book-cover-small">
  <div class="book-info">
    <h3>12. Barbarians at the Gate</h3>
    <div class="book-author">Bryan Burrough, John Helyar</div>
    <p class="book-notes">Ross Johnson of RJR Nabisco sure can spend money; as soon as I realized this book was telling the origin story of KKR, I knew it wouldn't end well for Johnson.</p>
  </div>
</div>

<div class="book-entry">
  <img src="https://covers.openlibrary.org/b/isbn/9780241357866-M.jpg" alt="The Dealmaker" class="book-cover-small">
  <div class="book-info">
    <h3>13. The Dealmaker</h3>
    <div class="book-author">Guy Hands</div>
    <p class="book-notes">Lessons from a life in private equity. Hands doesn't pull any punches when he tells about his failures. Career went off with a bang, then seemed to falter.</p>
  </div>
</div>

<div class="book-entry">
  <img src="https://covers.openlibrary.org/b/isbn/9780593135204-M.jpg" alt="Project Hail Mary" class="book-cover-small">
  <div class="book-info">
    <h3>14. Project Hail Mary</h3>
    <div class="book-author">Andy Weir</div>
    <p class="book-notes">The Martian was an instant classic, 2nd book was boring, but this 3rd title from Weir is fantastic. Fresh and fascinating scifi. First contact stories are my favorite and this one contains two!</p>
  </div>
</div>

<div class="book-entry">
  <img src="https://covers.openlibrary.org/b/isbn/9780060512804-M.jpg" alt="Cryptonomicon" class="book-cover-small">
  <div class="book-info">
    <h3>15. Cryptonomicon</h3>
    <div class="book-author">Neal Stephenson</div>
    <p class="book-notes">Cryptography is a powerful tool for both harm and for good. Doesn't top Snowcrash or Seveneves, but is one of Stephenson's best.</p>
  </div>
</div>
